Why Hollow Knight: Silksong?

This game means so much to me, after being confirmed as a sequel for Hollow Knight instead of a prequel title I just wanted more content even after I was done. What Is Hollow Knight: Silksong?

Hollow Knight: Silksong is the sequel to Hollow Knight, it explores the world of Pharloom inhabited by bugs that are plagued with the dream of wanting to go to the Citadel, their version of a holy land. But what the Citadel lies is far from holy with secrets and treasures to uncover as you go to the top to fight whatevers at the top. It feels like a dark souls game, it isn't a dark souls game, but it is still an RPG.









----Boss List----
List Of Silksong's Fights/Boss Enemies


----Moss Grotto (Act 1)----

The Moss Grotto is the first area you are introduced to in the game. It acts as the tutorial and home to many secrets you can backtrack too later, As well as the base of the Shamans.

Moss Mother

The Moss Mother is the first boss you fight. It's very simple as it's more tutorial esc and doesn't have any connections with the protagonist.

----Bone Bottom (Act 1)----

Bone Bottom, the pilgram filled village, where the journey to the holy land or The Citadel starts. It houses quests and acts as a hub of those who are still alive down here. You can fight a quest version of another boss here but it's nothing important to note.

----The Marrow (Act 1)----

The Marrow is the area after Bone Bottom. There isn't much to it then being the next area as the most notable thing about The Marrow is the Bell Beast boss used for transportation.

Bell Beast

This Bell Beast boss acts as the way of transportation throughout the game after you beat it. It's dog like actions have made the community adore this big shelled creature. At the end you learn that it has kids :3333.

Skull Tyrant

The Skull Tyrant is fought via quest which it's not a difficult boss and actually quite interesting and fun. It has no connection to the protagonist.

----Deep Docks (Act 1)----

The Deep Docks is the fourth area of the game you experience. Notable things about this area is a weapon and material trader for the games currency, late game stuff...AND LACE! A fan favorite due to her importance in the story. Though here her encounter feels random and surprising in a good way.

Lace

Lace is the daughter of Grand Mother Silk. Her encounter here feels random and surprising but due to events later like her second and final boss of the game fight, to the protagonist shes seen as a valuable companion and friend to be explored further in future post game content :).

Forebrothers Signis & Gron

This boss is pretty forgettable and annoying when first fought. It's a barrier between the Far Fields and connecting it to the Deep Docks. I got pretty mad fighting it due to it spawning the annoying enemies
of the area.

----Far Fields (Act 1)----

The Far Fields area has a lot going in if you think about all the area it connects to and end game content importance. It's a nice entrance in terms of the bosses to get to know the game with Karmelita being end game. By far the most notable thing in my opinion due to her music in the fight.

Fourth Chorus

The Fourth Chorus fight is SO FUN! It's genuinely one of the hooks for starting at the game. You learn more about what and where it comes from later in the game but it has no real connection to the progatgonist it's just really cool and fun.

----Hunters March (Act 1)----

Hunters March is regarded as a hard and dangerous area for early game due to the enemies as well as the new parkour system introduced. In Hollow Knight you'd do down attacks on specific world structures like spikes or enemies in order to parkour, in Silksong it's mostly just ball objects and some use enemies.

Savage Beastfly

I hate this. DAYS. It takes everyone DAYS or using cheap tricks to beat it. It's just not fun, it spawns a lot of enemies at once and it easily catches you off guard because of everything you need to look out for. It's neccesary to kill it for 100% so this made it even more annoying and angering.

----Greymoor (Act 1)----

Greymoor is a confusing labrinth of different paths and situations. Not the craziest most notable location but still works well to connect multiple things. If anything it's the aesthetic and depressing choice of the area, foggy, rainy, depressing overall. Yet somehow comforting...

Moorwing

This boss is annoying when first fought but by the time you beat it it's not terrible. It has no real effect in the game it just blocks you from entering Bellhart for the first time. Cool but no lore growth.

Crawfather

This fight just has every craw or bird like enemy is one boss room including the leader, The Crawfather. The Crawfather wasn't difficult to beat and doesn't mean anything for the game, it's an extra fight to get an endgame trophy thats more for succession then anything more.

----Shellwood (Act 1)----

Shellwood...is interesting for one particular reason. Theres an item you need for 100% that also unlocks a secret ending with the "final boss" of the game. The quest itself for the item is dark, unnerving, and overall unenjoyable as it just makes the game harder for a bit. The area itself without that factor is fun to traverse and important for other lore reasons.

Sister Splinter

Due to how long I took fighting the Savage Beastfly it was pretty easy to beat Sister Splinter due to similar fight strategy. The fight itself was very fun in a difficult and chaotic way though she only acts as a barrier to aquiring an important item.

----Bellhart (Act 1)----

The image shows a weirdness and unnerve to when you first enter Bellhart. It's called "Haunted Bellhart" due to all the citizens being hung up by webs created by an unknown entity who is the boss of this area. This entity is extremely important to the lore and once defeared, you get to see the lively but very small area of Bellhart start to feel like a home. Bellhart is the second hub in the game.

Widow

Widow is extremely important to the games lore. Them and Hornet are both weavers which was her mothers sides kin. They uncover lore that was already strongly hinted and is
the reason for Haunted
Bellhart's situation.

----Wormways (Act 1)----

The Wormways is parkous heavy like in the Hunters March area but it uses time limited platforms. Once you stand on them for over a few seconds they break while also having to dodge worms all over the area. There are now pits filled with these more violent and smaller versions that can't be killed.

Craggler

Craggler exists as an annoyance for getting to the Wormways from the top area of Bone Bottom. At first I thought it was unkillable with the damage it does and existance in the game. You need to be very careful in killing it as once it's dead it has it's smaller passive kin walk around.

Plasmified Zango

This is purely endgame and just a fun trial, it's the hardest boss to beat as in you need a perfect strategy. If your health is to low, you don't have the right specific tools, etc. Then it will keep healing itself at crazy amounts and doing crazy damage.

----Blasted Steps (Act 1)----

Blasted Steps is extremely annoying to traverse at first, a lot of annoying enemies, sandstorm mechanic that makes it difficult not to fall in the pits of violent tiny worms, and making it harder to parkour on this areas bell chains. Overall a very hateful experience until you get use to it with every miss or fall having the ability to cost you your life or your time.

Lost Garmond

Lost Garmond is the void infested/controlled version of a boss lower on this website. You gain him as a companion, a friend, trying to fight against the void with it's pal/pet Zaza. It was really sad to have to kill him as Zaza forever rests at his dead corpse.

Great Conchflies

The Great Conchflies is a pretty easy fight, it leads to one of the other bosses almost like a tragic loves story between the two. You kill one then the other comes back for revenge with their children. It's the barrier between the Sands Of Karak and the
Coral Tower.

Last Judge

The Last Judge was extremely annoying and took like 2 days to beat, yet still I don't hate it. It was a cool boss and the difficulty is what makes this game so satisying and enjoying. He stands as the guardian before the gate into the citadel.

----Grand Gate (Act 1)----

This tiny area has no boss, it is just the area after defeating Last Judge to gain access into The Citadel. It makes you believe that everything is smooth sailing...but that can't be farther from the truth.

----Underworks (Act 2)----

The Underworks is right after the Grand Gate, the elevator upwards to what should have been the next area "Choral Chambers" broke and sent itself all the way down here. As it's names it is the underbelly of the Citadel, where all the workers try their hardest to one day be recognized for the god who rules Pharloom. The bosses are strictly from the end game.

----Choral Chambers (Act 2)----

The Choral Chambers, although lively with workers, still feels so empty and useless. It's the connector to every area in the Citadel, so high importance in terms of the endgame or so I thought. The game has so much more content in store, but the Choral Chambers was still a nice place to wrap everything up nicely if you didn't want to partake in the secret last act.

Bell Eater

I don't dislike the Bell Eater because it's a hard fight or anything, I dislike the Bell Eater because I was afraid that the Bell Beast was dead because of it as I went to travel and encountered the Bell Eater. But thank god that wasn't the case or I would've cried real tears.

----Songclave (Act 2)----

The third and final hub in the game, like the other two it houses quests but more importantly the lore it contains for the true ending of the game.

----Cogwork Core (Lower) (Act 2)----

The Cogwork Core has two layers, the upper layer for closer to end game content, and this the lower layer with some secrets and surpirses to make yourself stronger. This area also introduces you to the difficulty of parkour moving forward, it did increase by a lot but still pretty fun. It gets you closer and closer to opening the elevator that leads you to the finale.

Cogwork Dancers

I LOVE THE COGWORK DANCERS! It relates to a whole side storyline of one of the characters, when you defeat one of the dancers the music becomes extremely depressing, as to then be understood when this storyline is revealed. This fight is really fun but depressing :(.

----Sinners Road (Act 2)----

Sinners Road is very annoying traverse, the enemeies make moving even one step untouched a difficult and impossible task. Spikes, projectiles...the M A G G O T S. Any type of water in this area is actually full of maggots, you move slower when in it and when you're out you're left with a maggot infection that unless you can heal it off you're stuck unable to heal.

Disgraced Chef Lugoli

There isn't anything good to say about this boss just crazy weird and gross. All if not most of the attacks can give you maggots and it attacks for two health points. Never again though it's needed for one of the major quests.

----Bilewater (Act 2)----

I hate it. I just hate it. This area isn't as annoying as one coming up but it was definitely in experience to behold. It houses the boss that made me start losing hope in beating the game any quicker then I was. It has a lot more maggot infested waters and parkour, so one wrong move means you get infected in an area that is difficult to heal in. It's also the area where you get the item needed for the quest in shellwood, the secret ending.

Groal The Great

This was by far the one boss I felt was genuinely impossible. There were so many rng related elements that could lead you to death in seconds. I had to use a workaround to beat it since it is important to kill them for the item they drop. Overall I hated
this creature...

----Weavenest Atla (Act 2)----

The Weavenests give lore and history to the protagonist's past. The area itself is has upgrade items for the player yet also has a returning boss from the start of the game, just twins.

Moss Mother Duo

The Moss Mother Duo isn't different then the Moss Mother boss, It's just two of them. It's quite forgetful and just there to cause a minor disruption to the journey. It doesn't have much substance either.

----The Mist (Act 2)----

The Mist is a very mysterious and secretive area, yet so pretty and pleasing. It's a maze like place where you follow white butterflies in order to reach the exit which leads you to the next area and secret boss fight, The Exhaust Organ.

----Exhaust Organ (Act 2)----

The Exhaust Organ, housing a classically composed and beautiful boss fight with the secret boss The Phantom. The area itself is small as it only really contains this. But the beauty, the stage, the organ itself, it's a masterpiece for whats to come.

Phantom

I genuinely love Phantom. In the lore Phantom is a failed experiment of making a being fully made of Silk like how Lace is, both created by Grand Mother Silk. It's a secret boss that gives you one of the best abilities that's extremely useful for endgame fights. Their theme is so damn good.

----The Slab (Act 2)----

Going into one of the old areas sends you to The Slab after getting kidnapped. It's an interesting cutscene that happens randomly, The area itself houses lore galore with the First Sinner. But even with this very funny circumstance, you must try to escape and continue with your journey with the annoying eneimes, annoying parkour, etc.

First Sinner

The First Sinner, a spider enemy that was corrupted by silk by Grand Mother Silk. Their hidden as a treacherous being kept in a cell until freed and defeated giving another very strong ability useful for whats to come. A little annoying but the theme is beautifully orchestrated.

Broodmother

The Broodmother is the creater of all the fly creatires in this area, so the boss itself is very unpleasent to look at and quite gross. It does not give an substancial plot to Hornet it just exists for quest purposes.

----Whispering Vaults (Act 2)----

The Whispering Vaults houses one of the Threefold Melodies you learn you need to continue up the elevator to the one who stands above all in Pharloom. The area also houses one of the fan favorite bosses Trobbio. Not much is very interesting here then it's connections to other areas.

Trobbio

The show master himself, with the dream to bring light and hope to the dark and hopeless Pharloom, Trobbio. Trobbio's fight is very flashy and enjoyable, you almost feel bad for him as when defeated he looks...miserable and shattered. Which makes sense to when we see him again.

Garmond & Zaza

This is who Lost Garmond was before getting corrupted by the void. Garmond and his pal/pet Zaza can be seen around the map fighting the void and having you join in. In the fight itself it was just a friendly spar match, really easy but sweet with his hope for Pharloom with no fear in his heart.

Tormented Trobbio

After Trobbio gave up on his dreams and wishes seeing that it wasn't gonna work out, he becomes the depressed Tormented Trobbio. It's harder then his original fight but still flashy, just more depressing and saddening.

----Mount Fay (Act 2)----

Mount Fay, the first parkour difficulty to gain one of the most quality of life items in the game to gain the ability to jump again in mid-air. It's quite difficult due to getting hypothermia in seconds and easily being able to die and have to restart. After getting to the top you meet the one who gives you the item...and adorable huge bird thing >:3.

Pinstress

The Pinstress is quite interesting. It's a act 3 only boss thats activated via quest. There isnt much to it, though it was quite an annoying fight but nothing on Groal The Great's level or Savage Beastfly.

----Whiteward (Act 2)----

Whiteward is the hospital like area in Silksong that houses the second secret boss, The Unravelled. The area itself is pretty annoying with the new type of enemy but overall it's too small to matter to much other then the secret bosses importance and quest importance.

The Unravelled

The Unravelled was extremely confusing and actually another boss that made me feel like it was impossible due to all the attacks it had. It was just a matter of learning and coming back to beat it. It's used for another important item for a quest leading up to endgame stuff.

----Memorium (Act 2)----

The Memorium is a terrarium like area that brings back memories to the journey and whats to come with all the mobs you've seen so far as well as new ones. It also connects to the area known as the Putrified Ducts...

----Putrified Ducts (Act 2)----

THIS is the most ANNOYING area in the game...theres this stupid flying mob that locks you into losing a lot of health because of how impossible it is to kill it quickly. Other then that it's a very maggot water filled area with creatures that make you wanna cry with it's annoyance. Though it does take you to the big secret lore drop ending...surrounding a mushroom.

----High Halls (Act 2)----

High Halls is extremely elagant and beautiful, it's putting the technological advancements and deadly aspects of The Citadel into a unified practice. Once you get all the way down you fight one of the most challenging boss rooms if you're not prepared to gain the second of the Threefold Melodies.

Second Sentinel

The robotic protector of the Citadel, fighting and defeating anything and anyone that wants to harm it. Hornet reawakens them, fights with them for awhile, then fights them when Second Sentinal believed you want to harm the Citadel. At the end though they become friends and ally's.

----Sands Of Karak (Act 2)----

Sands Of Karak is an area that can easily be missed. It's in the middle of nowhere but it's very interesting. It gives insight of the tribes that existed before the one above Pharloom began her tyrannical reign. This area is important for Act 3 and for now is not very needed yet still nice to explore to make it easier later.

Raging Conchfly

The Raging Conchfly fighting for revenge and the barrier between the Sands Of Karak and the Coral Tower. Fighting with itself and the other Conchfly's children the fight itself isn't very difficult but does take a minute due to the childrens line of attack. Overall is sad.

Voltvyrm

Voltvrym was EXTREMELY EASY for this stage of the game. It gives you an item that isn't that crazy in endgame but the boss itself is very forgettable with not that much importance and also very hidden.

----Wisp Thicket (Act 2)----

The Wisp Thicket is one of the final areas to encounter outside of heading into endgame. The boss here is amazing, it's nothing the game has showed before. The area itself is small as to why the boss is the highlight.

Father Of The Flame

This fight was amazing. The mechanic was super cool with the torches on its arm and being a stationary boss with difficult to dodge attacks. Genuinely another one of my favorites :3.

----Cogwork Core (Upper) (Act 2)----

This is the Cogwork Core's upper layer. It contains the last/third part of the Threefold Melody, now allowing for the activation of the elevator up to the "end of the game" where you finally meet the one above all. This area is only really note worthy for collecting the third part after completing its parkour section. The "end" is almost near.

----The Cradle (Act 2)----

The Cradle is the "final" area of the game, you finally meet the one above all, the god of Pharloom, Grand Mother Silk. We learn that Lace is her daughter as well as Phantom tho Phantom was a failed experiment to create a sentient being of Silk. The Cradle is inetersting for its lore and the tiny parkour section creating excitement and readiness to free Pharloom for good...unless you go for the true ending.

Lace

The second Lace fight ends in tragedy with Lace herself falling to the ground, telling Hornet that all she wanted was to be acknowledged by her mother and not be seen as a useless experimental failure...while her mother acknowledges Hornet instead.

Grand Mother Silk

God of Pharloom, controls all via silk, mother to Lace and Phantom, being her failed experiments to a being that can weild silk personally like Hornet. When defeated she gets taken by the void...as Lace sacrifices herself.

----Red Memory (Act 3)----

The Red Memory is a dream state you enter as you progress the games lore, you learn so much and also attain the item needed for the final boss of the true ending.

----Coral Tower and Sands Of Karak (Act 3)----

The Coral Tower is accessible though the Sands Of Karak after entering a dream world through Crust King Khann's corpse, the first figure of the clans before Grand Mother Silks reign. The area is only there for this fight but the structure itself and the new enemies make it feel like it's own thing. Sands Of Karak also has a new secret boss in Act 3.

Watcher At The Edge

In Act 3, the Watcher of the edge stands as an extra fight to get an endgame trophy thats more for succession then anything more like the Crawfather. It's quite troubling at first but it wasn't terrible.

Crust King Khann

The first figure of the clans before Grand Mother Silks reign, Crust King Khann. The boss fight itself you need to be alert as anything can catch you off guard and kill you. You must go through all the waves of enemies in the Coral Tower to access this and gain his heart.
<

----Underworks (Act 3)----

The endgame in the Underworks is accessable after breaking a wall. You fight the guardian to then fight Nytleth, the second figure of the clans before Grand Mother Silks reign. The area is nature heavy with nothing truly notable other then Nyleth's statue with words of praise and reverance.

Shrine Guardian Seth

The Shrine Guardian protecting over Nyleths statue and fight, Seth. Seth's fight is cool and interesting but particularly easy and simple. After he is defeated he goes on to live on
his life, with no duties and
no responsibilities.

Nyleth

The second figure of the clans before Grand Mother Silks reign, Nyleth. She protects everyone in her home of Shellwood as it's guardian who provides vegetation and protection. The Citadel acknowledged her and praise her. Defeating her grants the second needed heart.

----Far Fields (Act 3)----

The end game content in the Far Fields contains the aging yet living Skarrsinger Karmelita, the third figure of the clans before Grand Mother Silks reign. The area is quite expansive with it's new content and parkour heavy with it's clear connections to Hunters March.

Gurr The Outcast

Gurr The Outcast is as mentioned an outcast to the Skarr clan, reputable fighters and hunters. He's an outcast for hunting his own clan. Other then being here for that reason this fight isn't notable or provides much.

Skarrsinger Karmelita

The third figure of the clans before Grand Mother Silks reign, Skarrsinger Karmelita. One of the strongest bugs in all of Pharloom in her prime with a beuatiful singing voice. By far one of the hardest bosses IN THE GAME...yet the most profound.

----Lost Verdania (Act 3)----

As you beat the Cogwork Dancers from earlier you meet the prince you rescued in Sinners Road, this prince's home was Lost Verdania with his lover. It's quite pretty with different tests to progress as it exists to share his backstory and the glory that was Verdania in its prime.

Palestag

The Palestag is the treasure of the beings who reside in Verdania. Definitely a dodge heavy boss but nothing difficult or impossible.

Clover Dancers

The prince's lover created the Cogwork Dancers in their image. The fight has the prince and a ghost image of his lover as the reunited couple grants another trophy. It's pretty similar to the cogwork dancers and quite tragic with the prince dying after being defeated next to teh robotic image of his love.

----The Abyss (Act 3)----

The Abyss, the final area of the game, the final boss, Lost Lace. In the true ending, once you defeat Grand Mother Silk she is taken by the void, the counter energy/existance to the pale beings/gods. Lace gets corrupted and captures her mother, now you must beat and free Lace before it's too late.

Lost Lace

The true final boss, Lost Lace. She's extremely difficult as it took me the longest to beat her. After being consumed by the void she holds her mother down in the void as well. By the end Grand Mother Silk gives Hornwt the power to save herself and Lace, letting herself die to protect the one she should've cared for the most...

----Extra Bosses----

These 2 bosses are ones I did not fight. Shakra's fight is unknown to me as to how and where it coccurs, the Summoned Saviour is fought in the Soul Steel mode of the game, or the harder more hardcore version. It makes the game near impossible for many so I do not feel like partaking in it.

Shakra

Her fight is another sparring match that you can only have in Act 2. It's not difficult and looks fun but it's something I completely missed Shakra herself is an ally since the start who gives us the maps to progress the game, and a skilled fighter who rises to protect Bellhart from the void
in Act 3.

Summoned Saviour

The Summoned Saviour is in the hardcore mode of the game, Soul Steel. The boss itself is extremely hard with the upped difficulty of the mode, summoned by a bug who thought this to be a solution and protector to the wrath being faced in Pharloom.




Tier Lists

The Difficulty Tier List

The first tier list is for difficulty. The game itself is very difficult and patience reliant due to the different rng elements with what move the boss will do left, will you have time to heal, and does this boss have another phase that's going to steamroll you if you aren't careful.

The Appreciation Tier List

The second tier list is for appreciation. The game tho seen as difficult and off-putting becuase if it, theres so much beauty in the difficulty and the design. Due to it being an indie game company known as Team Cherry who did it, it takes time and love and the game got delayed god knows how many times because the studio was having too much fun...Anyway. The game is amazing, lovely, I'd say a masterpiece thats one of my favorite games EVER.
